# Knowledge Management - References

A curated collection of authoritative resources for Knowledge Management professionals.

---

## Books

### Foundational Knowledge Management

1. **The Knowledge-Creating Company: How Japanese Companies Create the Dynamics of Innovation**
   - Authors: Ikujiro Nonaka and Hirotaka Takeuchi
   - Publisher: Oxford University Press
   - URL: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-knowledge-creating-company-9780195092691
   - Description: Seminal work introducing the SECI model and the concept of knowledge creation as a key driver of innovation and competitive advantage.

2. **Working Knowledge: How Organizations Manage What They Know**
   - Authors: Thomas H. Davenport and Laurence Prusak
   - Publisher: Harvard Business Review Press
   - URL: https://hbsp.harvard.edu/product/1479-HBK-ENG
   - Description: Foundational text on practical knowledge management, covering knowledge markets, transfer, and organizational knowledge ecology.

3. **The Fifth Discipline: The Art and Practice of the Learning Organization**
   - Author: Peter M. Senge
   - Publisher: Currency Doubleday
   - URL: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/163984/the-fifth-discipline-by-peter-m-senge/
   - Description: Classic work on building learning organizations through systems thinking, personal mastery, mental models, shared vision, and team learning.

4. **Cultivating Communities of Practice: A Guide to Managing Knowledge**
   - Authors: Etienne Wenger, Richard McDermott, and William M. Snyder
   - Publisher: Harvard Business Review Press
   - URL: https://hbsp.harvard.edu/product/3308-HBK-ENG
   - Description: Definitive guide to understanding, designing, and cultivating communities of practice for knowledge sharing and organizational learning.

5. **Knowledge Management in Theory and Practice**
   - Author: Kimiz Dalkir
   - Publisher: MIT Press
   - URL: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262015080/knowledge-management-in-theory-and-practice/
   - Description: Comprehensive academic textbook covering KM theory, cycles, models, and practical implementation approaches.

### Knowledge Creation and Transfer

6. **Enabling Knowledge Creation: How to Unlock the Mystery of Tacit Knowledge and Release the Power of Innovation**
   - Authors: Georg von Krogh, Kazuo Ichijo, and Ikujiro Nonaka
   - Publisher: Oxford University Press
   - URL: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/enabling-knowledge-creation-9780195126167
   - Description: Explores the conditions and practices that enable knowledge creation, building on the SECI model.

7. **Managing Knowledge Work and Innovation**
   - Authors: Sue Newell, Maxine Robertson, Harry Scarbrough, and Jacky Swan
   - Publisher: Palgrave Macmillan
   - URL: https://www.macmillanihe.com/page/detail/Managing-Knowledge-Work-and-Innovation/?K=9780230522015
   - Description: Critical examination of knowledge work, innovation processes, and the management of knowledge-intensive organizations.

8. **Knowledge Management: An Integrated Approach**
   - Authors: Ashok Jashapara
   - Publisher: FT Prentice Hall
   - URL: https://www.pearson.com/en-gb/subject-catalog/p/knowledge-management-an-integrated-approach/P200000004267
   - Description: Integrative approach to KM covering strategic, organizational, and technical perspectives.

9. **Common Knowledge: How Companies Thrive by Sharing What They Know**
   - Author: Nancy M. Dixon
   - Publisher: Harvard Business Review Press
   - URL: https://hbsp.harvard.edu/product/4932-HBK-ENG
   - Description: Practical framework for understanding different types of knowledge transfer and matching appropriate methods.

10. **If Only We Knew What We Know: The Transfer of Internal Knowledge and Best Practice**
    - Authors: Carla O'Dell and C. Jackson Grayson
    - Publisher: Free Press
    - URL: https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/If-Only-We-Knew-What-We-Know/Carla-ODell/9780684844749
    - Description: APQC research-based guide to internal knowledge transfer and best practice sharing.

### Organizational Learning

11. **Organizational Learning: Creating, Retaining and Transferring Knowledge**
    - Author: Linda Argote
    - Publisher: Springer
    - URL: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-4614-5251-5
    - Description: Research-based examination of how organizations learn, retain, and transfer knowledge over time.

12. **Action Learning: New Techniques for Management**
    - Author: Reg Revans
    - Publisher: Blond & Briggs
    - URL: https://wial.org/action-learning/
    - Description: Original work on action learning methodology for organizational problem-solving and learning.

13. **The Knowing-Doing Gap: How Smart Companies Turn Knowledge into Action**
    - Authors: Jeffrey Pfeffer and Robert I. Sutton
    - Publisher: Harvard Business Review Press
    - URL: https://hbsp.harvard.edu/product/8890-HBK-ENG
    - Description: Explores why organizations fail to translate knowledge into action and how to bridge this gap.

14. **Dialogue and the Art of Thinking Together**
    - Author: William Isaacs
    - Publisher: Currency
    - URL: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/330863/dialogue-and-the-art-of-thinking-together-by-william-isaacs/
    - Description: Guide to creating dialogue for collective learning and knowledge creation in organizations.

15. **Organizational Learning II: Theory, Method, and Practice**
    - Authors: Chris Argyris and Donald A. Schon
    - Publisher: Addison-Wesley
    - URL: https://www.pearson.com/en-us/subject-catalog/p/organizational-learning-ii-theory-method-and-practice/P200000002836
    - Description: Foundational work on single-loop and double-loop learning in organizations.

### Complexity and Sense-Making

16. **Cynefin: Weaving Sense-Making into the Fabric of Our World**
    - Authors: Dave Snowden and friends
    - Publisher: Cognitive Edge
    - URL: https://thecynefin.co/cynefin-book/
    - Description: Comprehensive exploration of the Cynefin framework for sense-making and knowledge management in complex environments.

17. **Managing the Unknowable: Strategic Boundaries Between Order and Chaos in Organizations**
    - Author: Ralph D. Stacey
    - Publisher: Jossey-Bass
    - URL: https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Managing+the+Unknowable-p-9780470182895
    - Description: Explores complexity theory applications to organizational knowledge and strategy.

18. **Thinking in Systems: A Primer**
    - Author: Donella H. Meadows
    - Publisher: Chelsea Green Publishing
    - URL: https://www.chelseagreen.com/product/thinking-in-systems/
    - Description: Accessible introduction to systems thinking relevant to understanding knowledge flows and organizational dynamics.

### Knowledge Strategy and Implementation

19. **Designing Knowledge Management Architecture: How to Implement Successful Knowledge Management Programs**
    - Author: Archana Shukla
    - Publisher: Wiley
    - URL: https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Knowledge+Management-p-9780470827499
    - Description: Practical guide to designing and implementing knowledge management programs and architecture.

20. **Knowledge Solutions: Tools, Methods, and Approaches for Development**
    - Author: Olivier Serrat (Asian Development Bank)
    - Publisher: Springer Open
    - URL: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-981-10-0983-9
    - Description: Comprehensive collection of 100+ KM tools, methods, and approaches for organizational development.

## Papers and Research

### Foundational KM Research

21. **A Dynamic Theory of Organizational Knowledge Creation**
    - Author: Ikujiro Nonaka
    - Publication: Organization Science (1994)
    - URL: https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.5.1.14
    - Description: Seminal paper introducing the SECI model of organizational knowledge creation.

22. **Organizational Learning and Communities of Practice**
    - Authors: John Seely Brown and Paul Duguid
    - Publication: Organization Science (1991)
    - URL: https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.2.1.40
    - Description: Influential paper on situated learning and the role of communities in organizational knowledge.

23. **The Knowledge-Based View of the Firm**
    - Author: Robert M. Grant
    - Publication: Strategic Management Journal (1996)
    - URL: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/smj.4250171110
    - Description: Foundational paper establishing knowledge as the firm's most strategically significant resource.

24. **Tacit Knowledge, Knowledge Conversion, and Knowledge Spirals**
    - Authors: Ikujiro Nonaka and Ryoko Toyama
    - Publication: Organization Science (2003)
    - URL: https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.14.5.490.16862
    - Description: Advanced treatment of knowledge conversion processes and the concept of Ba.

25. **What's Your Strategy for Managing Knowledge?**
    - Authors: Morten T. Hansen, Nitin Nohria, and Thomas Tierney
    - Publication: Harvard Business Review (1999)
    - URL: https://hbr.org/1999/03/whats-your-strategy-for-managing-knowledge
    - Description: Influential article distinguishing codification and personalization strategies.

### Communities and Social Learning

26. **Communities of Practice: The Organizational Frontier**
    - Authors: Etienne C. Wenger and William M. Snyder
    - Publication: Harvard Business Review (2000)
    - URL: https://hbr.org/2000/01/communities-of-practice-the-organizational-frontier
    - Description: Introduces communities of practice to the management audience with practical guidance.

27. **Communities of Practice and Social Learning Systems**
    - Author: Etienne Wenger
    - Publication: Organization (2000)
    - URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/135050840072002
    - Description: Theoretical treatment of the relationship between communities and organizational learning.

28. **Knowledge Networks as Channels and Conduits**
    - Authors: Julia Eisenberg, Kathleen M. Carley, and Michael J. Farrell
    - Publication: Management Science (2005)
    - URL: https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.1050.0427
    - Description: Research on how network structures affect knowledge flow and organizational performance.

### Organizational Learning Research

29. **Organizational Learning**
    - Authors: Barbara Levitt and James G. March
    - Publication: Annual Review of Sociology (1988)
    - URL: https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/10.1146/annurev.so.14.080188.001535
    - Description: Classic review of organizational learning literature establishing key concepts.

30. **Absorptive Capacity: A New Perspective on Learning and Innovation**
    - Authors: Wesley M. Cohen and Daniel A. Levinthal
    - Publication: Administrative Science Quarterly (1990)
    - URL: https://www.jstor.org/stable/2393553
    - Description: Foundational paper on absorptive capacity as a determinant of organizational learning and innovation.

31. **A Knowledge-Based Theory of Interorganizational Collaboration**
    - Authors: Michael H. Zack
    - Publication: Strategic Management Journal (2003)
    - URL: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/smj.3145
    - Description: Research on knowledge transfer and collaboration between organizations.

### Complexity and Knowledge

32. **Complex Knowledge: Studies in Organizational Epistemology**
    - Author: Haridimos Tsoukas
    - Publication: Oxford University Press (2005)
    - URL: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/complex-knowledge-9780199275571
    - Description: Collection of papers on organizational knowledge from a complexity perspective.

33. **The Cynefin Framework: Applying Cognitive Theory to Understand Complexity**
    - Author: Dave Snowden
    - Publication: Knowledge Management Review (2002)
    - URL: https://cognitive-edge.com/articles/complex-acts-of-knowing-paradox-and-descriptive-self-awareness/
    - Description: Introduction of the Cynefin framework for categorizing problems by complexity.
