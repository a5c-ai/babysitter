# Physics - References

## Classical Mechanics

### Foundational Texts

**Landau and Lifshitz - Mechanics (Course of Theoretical Physics, Vol. 1):**
- Definitive treatment of classical mechanics
- Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations
- Principle of least action
- ISBN: 978-0750628969
- Reference: Landau, L.D. & Lifshitz, E.M. (1976) "Mechanics" Butterworth-Heinemann

**Goldstein - Classical Mechanics:**
- Comprehensive graduate-level textbook
- Extensive treatment of Lagrangian and Hamiltonian mechanics
- Canonical transformations and Hamilton-Jacobi theory
- ISBN: 978-0201657029
- Reference: Goldstein, H., Poole, C., & Safko, J. (2001) "Classical Mechanics" 3rd ed., Addison-Wesley

**Arnold - Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics:**
- Rigorous mathematical treatment
- Differential geometry approach
- Symplectic geometry and integrable systems
- ISBN: 978-0387968902
- Reference: Arnold, V.I. (1989) "Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics" 2nd ed., Springer

**Marion and Thornton - Classical Dynamics:**
- Undergraduate/early graduate level
- Clear explanations with many examples
- ISBN: 978-0534408961
- Reference: Marion, J.B. & Thornton, S.T. (2003) "Classical Dynamics of Particles and Systems" 5th ed., Brooks Cole

### Specialized Topics

**Chaos and Nonlinear Dynamics:**
- Strogatz, S.H. (2014) "Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos" 2nd ed., Westview Press
- ISBN: 978-0813349107
- Comprehensive introduction to nonlinear dynamics

**Celestial Mechanics:**
- Murray, C.D. & Dermott, S.F. (1999) "Solar System Dynamics" Cambridge
- ISBN: 978-0521575973
- Applications to planetary and satellite motion

**Fluid Mechanics:**
- Landau, L.D. & Lifshitz, E.M. (1987) "Fluid Mechanics" 2nd ed., Butterworth-Heinemann
- ISBN: 978-0750627672
- Theoretical foundations of fluid dynamics
